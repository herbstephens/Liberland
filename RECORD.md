## The Proposal

In early 2026, **Herb Stephens** (Co-Founder, Democracy Earth Foundation) engaged directly with **Vít Jedlička** (Founder and President, The Free Republic of Liberland) with a comprehensive proposal to adopt TIME Protocol as Liberland's foundational economic, governance, and citizenship layer.

The proposal had three pillars:

**1. Human Citizenship at Scale**  
Extend verifiable digital citizenship to Liberland's 735,000 applicants via World ID integration and TIME Protocol — giving each verified human a birthright TIME allocation and full governance participation rights. This would make Liberland, by verifiable democratic participation, more legitimate than most UN member states.

**2. The Global Tax Clearinghouse**  
Position Liberland as the world's most transparent tax routing infrastructure — collecting a micro-fee on every transaction settled through the protocol, and routing the remainder to the appropriate national treasuries automatically. Two independent revenue streams: (1) jurisdiction revenue when parties declare Liberland, and (2) infrastructure fees on all routed transactions regardless of declared jurisdiction.

**3. TIME Protocol as Economic Foundation**  
Replace or complement LLD with TIME Protocol as Liberland's native economic layer — a Bitcoin-backed, human-denominated token with a 24:1 maximum inequality ratio, 0.00% human-to-human fees, and governance staking mechanics.

Full technical documentation is contained in this repository.

## The Decision

President Jedlička declined to pursue the proposal. The declination rested on two stated positions:

---

### Objection 1: People = Corporations

President Jedlička holds, consistent with the reasoning of the US Supreme Court's *Citizens United v. FEC* (2010), that corporations are equivalent to persons and therefore entitled to the same rights as human citizens — including the right to incorporate in Liberland and operate free of taxation.

Liberland's current economic development strategy centers on attracting foreign corporations to register in Liberland as a zero-tax jurisdiction, generating revenue through registration fees.

**The response to this position** is documented in full in [`record/OBJECTIONS.md`](./OBJECTIONS.md) and in the standalone letter [`/record/CORRESPONDENCE.md`](./CORRESPONDENCE.md). The short form:

The corporate form is not a natural right. It is a state-granted legal fiction — specifically, the grant of limited liability, which caps shareholder downside while socializing losses onto workers, creditors, and communities. The libertarian tradition that Liberland claims to represent has never fully reconciled this. A true libertarian should be the *most* suspicious of granting state-created entities the same standing as human beings.

Citizens United did not expand freedom. Within 14 years, it had concentrated political power further in the hands of entities with unlimited capital and no stake in democratic outcomes. A Liberland corporate tax haven replicates this dynamic in the fiscal domain: corporations receive all the benefits of civilization and bear none of its costs — costs that are then transferred to the humans who cannot afford to restructure.

**This is not libertarian. This is cost-shifting.** And cost-shifting is, on libertarian terms, an act of aggression under the non-aggression principle.

---

### Objection 2: Misunderstanding of Universal Basic Income

President Jedlička expressed opposition to the birthright TIME allocation on the basis that it constitutes UBI — and that UBI is incompatible with Liberland's libertarian founding principles, as it represents redistribution without work.

**This objection rests on a misreading of the mechanism.**

TIME Protocol's birthright allocation is not redistribution. It is not funded by taxing the productive to pay the unproductive. It is an *issuance event* — new TIME created and assigned to a verified human, backed by the Bitcoin reserve, at a rate of 1 TIME per day of verified existence.

The correct analogy is not the welfare state. The correct analogy is **the homestead principle** — one of libertarianism's foundational concepts. Every human being, by virtue of existing, has a prior claim on their own time. They arrived before the market. The market did not create them. TIME Protocol makes this claim legible on-chain.

The 24 TIME/day ceiling — with 1 TIME birthright and up to 23 TIME earned through verified work — means the maximum inequality ratio across all TIME holders is **24:1**. This is not socialism. This is a protocol-level Gini coefficient that no other economic system, left or right, has ever achieved through mechanism rather than coercion.

Furthermore: Liberland's 735,000 citizenship applicants are, disproportionately, people who have chosen to seek out a new model. They are not seeking handouts. They are seeking dignity, participation, and recognition that their existence has value independent of the capital they have accumulated. Birthright TIME is that recognition — structured as a protocol primitive, not a government program.

## The Assessment

These two objections — people=corporations and opposition to birthright TIME — reflect a coherent internal logic. President Jedlička is consistent. He is applying a particular reading of libertarian philosophy with genuine conviction.

The argument of this repository is that this reading leads to the wrong destination: a Liberland that serves as a tax haven for corporate entities with no stake in the nation's future, governed by a philosophy that will concentrate power in exactly the way that Citizens United concentrated power in the United States — in the hands of entities that cannot die, do not need schools or hospitals, and will leave the moment a cheaper address is available.

The 735,000 humans waiting for Liberland deserve better than that.
