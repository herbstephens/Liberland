# Birthright TIME — Why This Is Not Charity

## The Objection

> *"Universal Basic Income is incompatible with libertarian principles. It is redistribution — taking from the productive to give to the unproductive. It creates dependency. It destroys the incentive to work. Liberland will not be a welfare state."*

This objection is coherent. It is also a misidentification of the mechanism.

Birthright TIME is not UBI in the welfare-state sense. It does not redistribute. It does not tax. It does not penalize productivity. It is something that libertarian philosophy has a name for — and that name is not "handout."

---

## The Homestead Principle

Murray Rothbard, whose work forms the intellectual backbone of much of modern libertarian property theory, articulated the homestead principle as follows: a person acquires legitimate ownership of unowned resources by mixing their labor with them. You clear the land; the land is yours. The prior investment of self creates a prior claim.

**Every human being has already homesteaded something before any market, employer, or government existed to evaluate it: their own time.**

You arrived before the market. You did not sign a contract to exist. Your time — the irreplaceable, non-renewable substrate from which all human labor is drawn — is yours by prior claim. The market did not create it. No employer granted it to you. It was yours before any exchange took place.

Birthright TIME makes this prior claim legible on-chain. It says: you were here. You are verified. Your existence — at the rate of one hour per day, denominated in the universal currency of human time — has value that precedes any market evaluation.

**This is not redistribution. This is recognition of what was already true.**

## What It Is Not Funded By

Traditional UBI schemes are funded by taxation — taking from those who have accumulated to provide a floor for those who have not. This is the version that correctly triggers libertarian objections. It involves coercion. It involves a third party — the state — determining what you owe and extracting it.

Birthright TIME is funded differently:

1. **Protocol issuance** — new TIME created by the protocol, backed by the Bitcoin reserve
2. **Bitcoin reserve** — accumulated from clearinghouse fee revenue (the infrastructure fee on transactions)
3. **Not a transfer** — no existing holder's TIME is reduced by birthright issuance

The analogy is not the welfare check. The analogy is the gold prospector who finds an unmined seam and files a claim. No one's gold is taken. New value is recognized and assigned to its rightful prior claimant.

## The Work Incentive Is Built In

A welfare payment is unconditional in perpetuity. It is the same whether you work or do not. This is the legitimate source of the dependency critique.

Birthright TIME is 1 TIME per day — one hour of a person's time, at market rate. Work TIME is up to 23 TIME per day, earned only when payment for work is received. The ceiling is 24 TIME.

The ratio is 1:23. For every unit of existence, there are 23 units of reward waiting for labor. **The protocol does not reward idleness. It rewards existence AND labor — in their correct proportions.**

A person who does nothing earns 1 TIME/day. A person who works earns up to 24 TIME/day. The incentive to work is 24× the incentive to exist. This is not a disincentive to work. It is a floor on destitution — denominated not in charity but in the recognition that your time has value even when no employer has chosen to pay for it yet.

---

## The 24:1 Cap Is Not Equality — It Is a Structure

The 24 TIME/day maximum does not make everyone equal. It makes the maximum ratio of wealth accumulation 24:1. A highly productive human with a full day of verified work earns 24× what an unverified, non-working human earns.

Compare this to existing economies:

- The US Gini coefficient (~0.49) implies wealth ratios orders of magnitude larger
- The top 1% hold ~32% of all US wealth
- CEO-to-worker pay ratios exceed 300:1 in the largest corporations

The 24:1 cap is not socialism. It is a protocol-level market structure that prevents the runaway concentration that has characterized every previous economic system — not by redistribution, but by design. The ceiling cannot be lobbied away. It is in the contract.

## What Libertarians Actually Oppose

The libertarian tradition opposes:
- **Coerced redistribution** — taking by force from one to give to another ✗ *(birthright TIME does not do this)*
- **Dependency creation** — unconditional payments that remove work incentives ✗ *(1:23 work:birthright ratio)*
- **State monopoly on welfare** — government as sole provider ✗ *(protocol-native, state-independent)*
- **Inflation via money printing** — creating currency without reserve backing ✗ *(100% Bitcoin-backed)*

Birthright TIME triggers none of these objections on examination. What it does do — recognizing that a human being's time has value before any employer has bid on it — is, on the deepest reading of libertarian property theory, **exactly what that theory requires.**

## The Practical Argument

Set philosophy aside entirely. Consider the practical question:

Liberland has 735,000 applicants. Most of them are not wealthy. Many are from developing economies where the existing system has given them no path to economic dignity. They applied to Liberland because they believed it represented something new.

Birthright TIME gives each of them, upon World ID verification:
- A retroactive lump sum equal to their age in years × 365 TIME
- A daily allocation of 1 TIME going forward
- Full governance participation through TIME staking
- A Work NFT history starting from their first verified transaction

This is not charity. It is the foundational act of a nation recognizing its citizens. Every nation does this — they just do it through passports, voting rights, and public services. Liberland does it through TIME Protocol, transparently, on-chain, without bureaucracy.

The only difference is that Liberland's version is auditable, automatic, and cannot be revoked by a future administration.

## The Question for Vít

If birthright TIME is welfare, then what is the $10,000 passport fee that currently locks 99.8% of Liberland's applicants out of citizenship?

It is a price. And any price set high enough becomes a filter — selecting for capital over conviction, for inherited wealth over genuine belief in the project. The 735,000 who applied without paying $10,000 may be more committed to Liberland's future than any corporation that registers for a fee.

Birthright TIME is the mechanism by which Liberland can say to those 735,000: **you don't need $10,000. You need a face and an iris scan. Your existence is enough to begin.**

That is not a welfare state. That is a nation worth founding.
