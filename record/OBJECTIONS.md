# The Two Objections — And the Responses

**A record of the specific positions on which the proposal was declined, and the case against each.**

---

## Objection 1: People = Corporations (The Citizens United Position)

### The Position

President Jedlička holds that corporations are persons — that they are entitled to the same rights as human citizens, including the right to incorporate in Liberland and operate without taxation. This is consistent with the reasoning of the US Supreme Court's *Citizens United v. FEC* (2010), which held that corporate spending is a form of protected speech equivalent to individual expression.

On this basis, Liberland's economic development strategy centers on attracting foreign corporations to register in Liberland as a zero-tax jurisdiction.

### Why This Is Wrong on Libertarian Terms

**First: The corporation is a state creation.**

The defining feature of the modern corporation is limited liability — the legal doctrine that shareholders are not personally responsible for corporate debts and harms beyond their invested capital. This doctrine does not exist in nature. It was created by government legislation in the mid-19th century specifically to encourage capital formation.

Without that government grant, there are no corporations. There are only partnerships — where every owner is personally and fully liable for everything. The corporation's most valuable property — the cap on personal loss — is a state subsidy. A libertarian who defends corporate personhood is defending a state-granted privilege, not a natural right.

**Second: The empirical record of Citizens United.**

The decision was handed down in January 2010. By the 2020 election cycle, corporate and dark money groups — the majority incorporated in low-disclosure jurisdictions — had spent over $1 billion in a single election, with donors legally concealed. By 2024, the number exceeded $4 billion in disclosed spending alone.

The human voter did not gain a voice. The human voter's voice was diluted by entities with resources no human can match, no mortality to account for their decisions, and no stake in the communities they affected.

Citizens United did not expand freedom. It transferred political power from humans to capital structures. A Liberland corporate tax haven replicates this transfer in the fiscal domain.

**Third: Cost-shifting is not freedom.**

A corporation that incorporates in Liberland to escape taxes in Germany continues to use German roads, German courts, German universities that trained its workforce, German hospitals that keep its employees healthy, and the German legal system that enforces its contracts. The cost of these things does not disappear. It is transferred — to the German worker who cannot restructure, the German homeowner who pays property taxes, the German small business that cannot afford Dublin offices.

On the non-aggression principle — foundational to libertarian ethics — imposing costs on others without consent is an act of aggression. Tax avoidance via corporate restructuring is cost-shifting. Cost-shifting without consent violates the NAP. **A true libertarian should oppose it.**

**Fourth: What Liberland loses.**

Liberland has one asset that no existing nation has: the credibility of being genuinely new, genuinely principled, and genuinely different. The moment Liberland becomes a corporate tax haven, it forfeits that asset permanently.

It becomes one of dozens of low-disclosure incorporation jurisdictions — competing with Cayman, BVI, and Luxembourg for corporate registrations while the OECD Global Minimum Tax and FATCA progressively strangle the model. It is starting the race 40 years late, in a race that is already being called off.

## Objection 2: Birthright TIME = UBI = Incompatible with Libertarianism

### The Position

President Jedlička expressed opposition to the birthright TIME allocation on the basis that it constitutes Universal Basic Income — redistribution without work, incompatible with Liberland's founding principles of voluntary exchange and non-coercion.

### Why This Misreads the Mechanism

**First: Birthright TIME is a homestead claim, not a transfer payment.**

The homestead principle — foundational to libertarian property theory as articulated by Rothbard and others — holds that a person acquires rights to unowned resources by mixing their labor with them. The paradigmatic case is the settler who improves land: the prior investment creates a prior claim.

Every human being has homesteaded something before any market existed to price it: **their own time.** Your existence preceded any employer, any market, any contract. Your time — the irreplaceable substrate from which all labor is drawn — is yours by prior claim. Birthright TIME makes this claim legible on-chain. It is recognition, not redistribution.

**Second: Birthright TIME is not funded by taxation.**

Traditional UBI is funded by taxing productive people to provide a floor for unproductive people. This is the version libertarians correctly oppose: it involves coercion, third-party extraction, and the destruction of work incentives.

Birthright TIME is funded by:
- **Protocol issuance** — new TIME created by the protocol at the moment of verification
- **Bitcoin reserve** — accumulated from clearinghouse infrastructure fee revenue
- **Not a transfer** — no existing holder's TIME is reduced by birthright issuance

No one is taxed. No redistribution occurs. The Bitcoin reserve is built from transaction fee revenue — the same infrastructure that makes the clearinghouse work. Birthright TIME is backed by the value the protocol creates, not by the labor of other citizens.

**Third: The work incentive is built into the ratio.**

Welfare payments are often the same regardless of whether you work. This creates the dependency dynamic libertarians correctly critique.

Birthright TIME: 1 TIME/day.  
Work TIME: up to 23 TIME/day.

For every unit of existence, there are 23 units of reward for labor. The incentive to work is 24× the incentive to merely exist. This is not a disincentive. It is a floor on destitution combined with an enormous reward for participation.

**Fourth: The practical consequence of the objection.**

The current pathway to Liberland citizenship costs $10,000. This price point locks out 99.8% of the 735,000 applicants — the vast majority of whom are from countries where $10,000 represents a year's income or more.

The result is that Liberland's citizenship base is filtered by wealth, not conviction. The people most committed to the project, most likely to contribute to its future, most likely to become genuine residents and participants — are excluded by a price they cannot pay.

Birthright TIME, triggered by World ID verification, costs nothing to claim. Your face and your iris are enough. Your existence is the qualification.

If President Jedlička's concern is dependency, the question worth asking is: which creates more dependency — giving a person 1 TIME/day as a birthright floor, or requiring a $10,000 payment before they are permitted to participate at all?

## The Underlying Disagreement

Both objections reflect a consistent internal logic. President Jedlička applies a particular reading of libertarian philosophy: that free markets require minimal intervention, that corporations and persons are equivalent rights-holders, and that any guaranteed allocation without prior work constitutes welfare.

This reading is coherent. The argument of this repository is that it leads to conclusions that libertarian philosophy, properly applied, does not support:

1. Corporations are not natural persons — they are state-granted legal fictions. Defending their "rights" as equivalent to human rights is not libertarian. It is corporate capture wearing libertarian language.

2. Birthright TIME is not redistribution. It is the recognition of a prior claim that predates any market. The failure to recognize it is not neutral — it is a choice to define personhood by accumulated capital rather than by existence.

The 735,000 people waiting for Liberland deserve a leadership that makes the correct choice.
