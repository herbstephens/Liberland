# Correspondence and Engagement Timeline

**A chronological record of the engagement between Herb Stephens (Democracy Earth Foundation) and Vít Jedlička (Liberland), 2025–2026.**

---

## Background

Herb Stephens and Vít Jedlička share a long-running intellectual connection through the overlapping worlds of blockchain governance, libertarian political theory, and the construction of new institutions. Both have worked, from different angles, on the question of what a legitimate digital-native polity looks like.

The engagement that produced this repository emerged from that shared context — a genuine attempt by Herb Stephens to bring Democracy Earth Foundation's decade of work on proof-of-humanity, quadratic voting, and economic primitives to bear on the most concrete opportunity available: a real nation, with real citizens, at the exact moment when its economic architecture was still being decided.

## Timeline

### Early 2026 — Lisbon Meeting

Herb Stephens met Vít Jedlička in person in Lisbon, Portugal. This was the primary in-person engagement during which the proposal was discussed directly.

The meeting covered:
- TIME Protocol's architecture and the dual-stream clearinghouse model
- The distinction between human citizenship and corporate registration
- The Citizens United parallel and its implications for Liberland's governance model
- The objection to birthright TIME as UBI and the homestead principle response

**Outcome:** President Jedlička expressed interest in TIME Protocol as a technical layer but maintained his position that corporate tax haven registration was central to Liberland's economic development strategy, and that birthright TIME was incompatible with his reading of libertarian principle.

## The Two Positions

### Herb Stephens (Democracy Earth Foundation)

**On corporations:** Corporations are not citizens. They are state-created legal fictions — granted limited liability by government statute, not by natural right. Treating them as equivalent to human persons in Liberland's governance and economic architecture will replicate the outcome of Citizens United: concentrated power in entities with unlimited capital and no stake in human outcomes.

**On birthright TIME:** Birthright TIME is not UBI in the welfare-state sense. It is a homestead claim — recognition that human existence precedes and is independent of market valuation. It is funded by protocol issuance backed by Bitcoin reserves, not by redistribution from productive citizens. The 1:23 ratio of birthright to work TIME maintains strong incentives for labor.

**On the clearinghouse:** The corporate tax haven model is a 40-year-old product being actively dismantled by OECD mechanisms that Liberland has no capacity to resist. The clearinghouse model generates more revenue, serves more governments, earns more moral authority, and is immune to the political pressure that has already broken every other tax haven.

### Vít Jedlička (Liberland)

**On corporations:** Corporations are persons — associations of humans with equivalent rights to individual humans. This is consistent with the Citizens United reasoning. Foreign corporations should be able to incorporate in Liberland and operate tax-free. Registration fees are a viable revenue model.

**On birthright TIME:** Universal Basic Income is incompatible with libertarian principles. Any guaranteed allocation without prior work constitutes redistribution and creates dependency. Liberland will not be a welfare state.

## What Was Not Disputed

For the record, there was substantial agreement on:
- The importance of blockchain-native governance
- The value of quadratic voting as a mechanism
- The strategic importance of Liberland's 735,000 citizenship applicants
- World ID as a compelling Sybil-resistance mechanism
- The general direction of TIME Protocol as an interesting economic primitive

The disagreement was specifically on:
1. Whether corporations should be treated as citizens
2. Whether birthright TIME constitutes impermissible redistribution

## A Note on Tone

This record is not adversarial. President Jedlička built something remarkable. Liberland exists — physically, legally, and philosophically — because of his conviction and effort. The disagreement documented here is genuine and deep, but it is a disagreement between people who share a commitment to building something better than the current system.

The hope of this repository is that when Liberland's next chapter begins — whether under new leadership or under a President Jedlička who has revised his view — the blueprint will be here, complete and ready.
